PROMPT: Implement `train_rl_agents.py` — Production-Grade, Safety-First RL Training Pipeline for Grid-Guardian

Goal (one line)
Build `train_rl_agents.py`, a production-quality training pipeline that constructs a Gym-like microgrid environment from the canonical Grid-Guardian dataset, trains multiple baseline agents (including modern sequence-model offline RL), enforces a safety-shield, evaluates with OPE & stress tests, and packages safe policies for Raspberry Pi deployment. The code must be reproducible, well-documented and include unit/integration tests.

---

ENVIRONMENT & DEPENDENCIES (must run)

* Python 3.10+ (Linux/macOS/Windows). GPU optional.
* Required libs (pip): `numpy pandas gymnasium torch torchvision stable-baselines3 sb3-contrib tianshou transformers datasets optuna scikit-learn pyyaml hydra-core wandb tensorboard onnx onnxruntime`
* Offline-specific libs: `d3rlpy` or `acme`/`rlkit` (optional) and `fqe`/`d4rl` tools if available.
* Provide `requirements.txt`, Dockerfile (CPU & GPU), and a small conda environment example.

High-level design requirements (must implement)

1. **Gym-like microgrid environment** (`env/`): implements Gymnasium API; supports dataset-driven replay wrapper (offline), vectorized envs, fault injection (cloud ramps, outages, EV spikes), and a safety-check wrapper (safety shield) that enforces hard constraints pre-action.
2. **Agents**: include BC baseline, classical RL (DQN/PPO/SAC/DDPG), conservative/offline algorithms (CQL, BCQ, BRAC), and **sequence-based offline RL** (Decision Transformer / Trajectory Transformer). Provide integrations using stable-baselines3, tianshou, d3rlpy, and HuggingFace Transformers where appropriate.
3. **Replay buffer & dataset interface**: convert `generated_dataset.csv.gz` → transitions `(s, a, r, s', done, info)` and provide utilities to create mini-batches, prioritized sampling, reservoir sampling and sequence trajectories for Decision Transformer. Support prefill + online hybrid.
4. **Safety shield**: policy wrapper that checks proposed action against hard constraints (SoC limits, max charge/discharge, grid draw limits) and either: (a) block/clip action; (b) replace with safe fallback rule-based action; (c) return rejection with warning. This shield must be configurable and independent of agent.
5. **Conservative training practices**: penalize KL divergence to behavior policy, clip Q-values, and integrate offline-safe algorithms (CQL/BRAC). Provide BC behavior policy estimator (behavior cloning) used for penalties & OPE.
6. **Sequence-model RL**: implement Decision Transformer training + inference (sequence of states, actions, returns-to-go). Support variable-length history (configurable 12–48 timesteps) and sampling of trajectories from the dataset. Compare DT performance with other baselines.
7. **Domain randomization & forecast-noise injection**: during training and evaluation, randomly perturb forecasts, latency, inverter efficiency, and sensor noise. Domain randomization must be a first-class config option and applied per-episode (not only in evaluation).
8. **Offline Policy Evaluation (OPE)**: implement IS/WIS, FQE, Doubly Robust; produce confidence intervals and conservative lower-bounds for policy value. Use OPE to screen candidate policies before any simulated online evaluation.
9. **Stress tests & safety evaluation**: run held-out tests and adversarial scenarios (cloud ramps, outages, sensor dropout) and produce safety metrics (safety_violation_rate, SoC violations, CVaR of cost).
10. **Packaging & edge export**: convert best policy to TorchScript and ONNX, provide quantization guidance, and a minimal runtime loader for Raspberry Pi 5 (ARM64). Include model input schema & normalization.

Training loop & conservative measures (must implement)

* For offline mode:

  * Train CQL/BCQ/BRAC or DT on the replay buffer.
  * When using algorithms not inherently offline-safe (SAC/PPO), add behavior-penalty regularizer: `loss += λ * KL(pi || pi_behavior)` with behavior policy estimated by BC.
  * Periodically evaluate candidate policies using OPE and on short held-out test episodes (simulator rollouts only if safe).
* For online/hybrid mode:

  * Pre-fill buffer with dataset, then run environment rollouts and continue training with constrained exploration (low epsilon / low noise), with safety shield active.
* For Decision Transformer:

  * Train on trajectories with return-to-go conditioning; evaluate by sampling autoregressively and applying safety shield.

Evaluation & metrics (must produce)

* Learning curves (episodic reward), cost_savings, energy_sold, energy_bought.
* Safety metrics: safety_violation_rate, SoC_violation_count, episodes_with_violation.
* OPE estimates: IS/WIS/FQE/DR with confidence intervals; conservative lower bound for policy value.
* Robustness: test under domain randomized episodes and adversarial stress (cloud ramp %, outage durations, high EV spikes).
* Action distribution drift: KL(pi_new || pi_behavior) over time.
* Compute worst-case (5th percentile) reward and CVaR.

Safety Shield (exact behavior, must implement)

* Modular wrapper that can be inserted between agent and env. Modes:

  * `clip`: clip action to safe thresholds (soc bounds, power limits).
  * `fallback`: replace action with safe rule-based fallback (e.g., maintain soc margin or charge when price low).
  * `reject`: reject unsafe actions and log an incident; return last safe action.
* Shield must be independent, unit-tested, and configurable per-household.

Sequence RL specifics (Decision Transformer)

* Tokenization: discretize actions if needed for DT or use continuous conditional modeling.
* Data preparation: create rolling trajectories, compute returns-to-go, normalize states, and include masked tokens for variable lengths.
* Architecture: GPT-style transformer with causal attention, positional encodings, MLP heads for actions.
* Training tips: short warm-up, small batch sizes for DT, gradient clipping, learning rate schedule.
* Inference: generate actions autoregressively from last K steps plus desired return-to-go; then apply safety shield.
