# Grid-Guardian — Blockchain Workflow (comprehensive, step-by-step)

Short executive summary
This document describes a production-grade, end-to-end blockchain workflow for Grid-Guardian: how offers are created on Raspberry Pi edge nodes, how matching and settlement happen on a blockchain, what stays off-chain, how privacy and safety are enforced, and which modern tools/protocols to use to achieve low latency, scalability, and auditability. It is chronological and implementation-focused (contracts, off-chain services, Pi integration, security, testing and operations). See the system architecture reference for the overall picture. 

---

## Highest-level design choices (quick)

* **On-chain:** authoritative settlement, escrow, identity registry, trade records, minimal proofs of integrity.
* **Off-chain / edge:** negotiation, matching optimizer (optional), price discovery, telemetry aggregation, heavy cryptography (ZK) generation.
* **Ledger choice:** Ethereum-compatible **L2** (zkEVM or optimistic rollup) or a permissioned consortium chain for pilots (e.g., Hyperledger Besu). Use L2 mainnet (Polygon zkEVM / Optimism / Arbitrum Nitro) for public pilots to keep costs low and tooling mature.
* **Payment rail:** stablecoin on L2 (USDC or equivalent).
* **Privacy:** commit–reveal + selective ZK proofs (generated off-device, verified on chain) for constraint compliance.
* **Edge node:** Raspberry Pi runs a light Web3 client or uses a secure gateway (recommended) to avoid full node resource needs.

## Chronological execution timeline — step by step

### 0) Preliminaries — identities, onboarding, deposits

1. **Node registration (one-time).**

   * Pi generates an ECDSA keypair (Ethereum-style); private key stored in HSM/secure element (ATECC/TMP) or encrypted keystore. Public key + metadata (household_id, archetype, attestation) are submitted to the **Identity Smart Contract (IdentitySC)** which mints or records the node identity and on-chain certificate.
   * Optionally, register off-chain KYC/consent in a permissioned registry if required by regulation.

2. **Collateral deposit / credit setup.**

   * Node funds an on-chain escrow account (or posts deposit) in stablecoin to demonstrate solvency for trades. This deposit is held by the **Escrow Smart Contract**.

> Outcome: each Pi has an on-chain identity and required collateral to participate.

---

### 1) Edge sensing → offer creation (edge → off-chain signing)

1. **Local sensing & decision (edge).**

   * Pi collects sensor data, runs local AI (forecast & decision). If auction/market participation is decided, TC composes an offer: `{ts, household_id, offered_kwh, price_per_kwh, validity_window, offer_nonce}`.

2. **Local commitment & signing (edge).**

   * Pi calculates `offer_hash = sha256(ts|household_id|offered_kwh|price|nonce)` and stores salt locally at `secrets/<household>/<offer_id>.salt` (POSIX 600). It signs `offer_hash` with the private key (EIP-191/EIP-712 compliant signature).
   * If commit–reveal is used, the Pi first publishes the `commit_hash = sha256(offer_hash|salt)` (or just `offer_hash`) as the commit on-chain or to an off-chain bulletin.

3. **Broadcast offer (fast path: off-chain P2P).**

   * Pi broadcasts the offer to local neighbors via libp2p / MQTT (gossip). This reduces latency for immediate local trades and enables local matching.
   * Simultaneously (or on scheduled cadence) Pi submits the offer to the **Trading Smart Contract (TSC)** via a Web3 RPC call (see step 2). For on-chain submission, the Pi sends a **lightweight on-chain transaction** that includes either the commit_hash (preferred) or a compact offer payload.

> Security: never include the salt or raw private telemetry in on-chain fields. Only commit hashes, offer metadata, and signatures are included.

---

### 2) Offer collection & market clearing (blockchain or hybrid)

Two deployment patterns are valid; choose per pilot needs:

**A. Fully on-chain matching (deterministic, most transparent)**
4A. **Offers accumulate on chain.**

* TSC stores the submitted (committed) offers in an on-chain order book or ring buffer for the current clearing round. Gas optimization: store only compact structs (seller_id, quantity, price_bucket, commit_hash, expiry_ts).
  5A. **Market clearing smart contract run.**
* At scheduled clearing times (hourly, sub-hourly) the TSC executes a deterministic clearing algorithm (e.g., uniform price auction) using on-chain data. Because this can be heavy, either:

  * keep matching simple (price bucket matching) on-chain; or
  * call a **trusted off-chain optimizer** (see B) and publish results via a signed oracle call.
    6A. **Commit–reveal (if used).**
* During reveal window participants reveal salt on-chain (or via a reveal transaction to TSC). TSC verifies `sha256(offer_hash|salt) == commit_hash` and accepts the revealed offer values.

**B. Hybrid off-chain optimizer with on-chain settlement (recommended for performance)**
4B. **Offers posted on chain as commits; off-chain matching optimizer reads commits (or listens to P2P offers).**
5B. **Off-chain optimizer (trusted or decentralized) runs the heavy LP/market algorithm.**

* Optimizer produces a deterministic matching result and posts a signed **MatchResult** to an on-chain **Match Registry Contract**.
* Alternatively, use a state channel or a rollup aggregator to bundle matching results for batch settlement.
  6B. **Smart contract verifies optimizer signature and posts final settlement transactions.**

> Note: hybrid pattern delivers faster, cheaper matching while retaining on-chain settlement & audit trail. Use cryptographic signing and nonces to prevent replay/forgery.

---

### 3) Settlement & escrow (on-chain)

7. **Locking funds & match acceptance.**

   * When match posted, TSC (or SettlementSC) checks buyer collateral, locks necessary funds in escrow, and emits `TradeProposed` events.
8. **On-chain confirmation / finalization.**

   * After a short settlement notice (and optional reveal verification), TSC finalizes trades, transfers funds from buyer escrow to seller escrow, or records a pending settlement to be completed after physical delivery confirmation.
9. **Event emission.**

   * TSC emits events `TradeExecuted(trade_id, buyer, seller, qty, price)` for watchers.

---

### 4) On-chain → edge actuation & delivery confirmation

10. **Pi listens for events.**

    * Each Pi runs a lightweight client or connects to a gateway/relayer that filters on `TradeExecuted` events for that node’s identity.
11. **Local fine-control + safety shield.**

    * Pi’s SC translates contract result into hardware actions: discharge battery, throttle load, or accept grid draw. Before action, the safety shield runs final checks (SoC, inverter, line limits).
12. **Delivery reporting.**

    * Pi records actual energy exchanged (meter snapshot) and pushes a signed **DeliveryReceipt** to the chain (or to an off-chain aggregator with an on-chain anchor).
13. **Escrow release.**

    * On verification of delivery (either automatic via TSC rules or via dispute window), escrow is released and funds transferred to seller.

---

### 5) Audit, disputes, and proofs of integrity

14. **Audit trail storage.**

    * All trade records, commit hashes, delivery receipts and settlement events remain on chain. Aggregated telemetry may be anchored (hash) on IPFS or on chain for additional auditability.
15. **Dispute resolution.**

    * If buyer disputes delivery, a dispute procedure triggered: both parties submit meter snapshots + signed telemetry. TSC (or an adjudicator DAO) resolves disputes; on-chain governance rules define penalties.
16. **Proofs of integrity / ZK.**

    * For privacy-sensitive verification (e.g., verify that the sold energy did not exceed local line capacity or the agent obeyed safety constraints) the Pi (or a trusted proving service) generates a zkSNARK/PLONK that demonstrates compliance without revealing private usage. The proof or its hash is submitted on-chain for verification by a verifier smart contract.

---

### 6) MLOps loop & model updates

17. **Telemetry aggregation & retraining triggers.**

    * Periodic aggregates (anonymized/hashed) are collected (off-chain) to MLOps servers. If concept drift detected, retrain models in offline ML environment.
18. **Model package & secure rollout.**

    * New model packaged and signed (code signing). Pi verifies model signature on update before activation. On-chain event can announce major model rollouts for audit.

## What to keep on-chain vs off-chain (guidelines)

* **On-chain (store permanently):** identity, collateral balance state, commit_hashes, minimal offer index, trade executed events, settlement outcomes, dispute outcomes, proof hashes.
* **Off-chain (store raw & heavy):** raw telemetry, salts, full meter logs, heavy zk proof generation, optimizer runs. Anchor summaries (hashes) on-chain when provenance is required.

## Smart contract architecture (recommended contracts & APIs)

1. **IdentitySC**

   * `registerNode(pubkey, metaURI)` → records household details & authorization.
   * `revokeNode(node)` → governance removal.

2. **Collateral/WalletSC** or integrate into IdentitySC

   * `deposit(node, amount)`
   * `withdraw(node, amount)` (with cooldown)
   * `lockFunds(trade_id, buyer, amount)` / `unlockFunds(...)`

3. **TradingSC (Order book & settlement)**

   * `postCommit(node, commit_hash, expiry_ts)` — store commit reference.
   * `revealOffer(node, offer_data, salt)` — optional reveal verification.
   * `finalizeMatching(match_signed_by_optimizer)` — accept optimizer result or run on-chain matching.
   * `executeSettlement(trade_id)` — move funds from buyer escrow to seller.

4. **MatchRegistry (for hybrid)**

   * `submitMatch(signedMatchBlob)` → accepts signed off-chain optimizer output for settlement.

5. **ProofVerifierSC (optional)**

   * `verifyProof(proof_blob)` → returns boolean/score; used to verify zk proofs onchain.

6. **Governance / DisputeSC**

   * `raiseDispute(trade_id, evidence)`, `resolveDispute(...)` etc.

Security & access control:

* Use OpenZeppelin libraries (Ownable, AccessControl), role-based permissions for oracles/optimizers during pilot.
* Use gas-efficient data packing and emit events rather than store large structs.

## Off-chain services & components (details)

* **Relayer / RPC gateway:** A secure, scalable gateway (AWS/GCP/edge) that relays events to Pis, provides archive RPC endpoints, and batches on-chain writes. Pis can use this instead of running full clients.
* **Matching optimizer / Market operator:** Off-chain optimizer runs LP/auction, signs result. Can be decentralized later via multiple auditors or MPC.
* **Oracle service:** Provide external data (utility TOU price, weather anchor) via Chainlink or a custom oracle. Oracles can feed price caps or reference prices used in clearing.
* **Proof generation service (ZK prover farm):** Performs expensive ZK proof computation for Pis that cannot. Generates proofs and posts to chain or stores proof hash.
* **Monitoring & indexer:** The indexer listens to events, maintains a trade database for UIs and MLOps. Also used for dispute evidence.

## Edge (Pi) integration patterns (best practices)

* **Do not run full node on Pi.** Run a light client or use a trusted relayer/gateway with signed JSON-RPC over TLS. Light client options: Geth light mode or use a remote archive node with local caching.
* **Signing model:** Use local signing on Pi (private key never leaves device). Use secure element (ATECC608A) if possible. If not, use encrypted keystore with TPM or passphrase.
* **Salt handling:** Salt files remain local and must never be uploaded. The Pi reveals salt only when required for commit–reveal; uploading salt to an untrusted server is forbidden.
* **Retry & idempotency:** Use nonces and idempotency keys for on-chain submissions. Maintain local queue for retries and a record of tx hashes for each offer.

## Privacy & zk-proof pattern (recommended)

1. **Commit–reveal baseline** for privacy of exact meter values.
2. **ZK proof for rule compliance:** Pi proves `sum(sent_energy) <= measured_export` and `did not exceed line capacity` without revealing exact usage. Prover runs off-device; verifies on chain.
3. **Proof workflow:** Pi uploads proof blob to proof service (or generates locally), service publishes proof hash on chain; ProofVerifierSC validates succinct proof (verifier code on chain) and emits `ProofVerified(trade_id)` event.

Note: ZK is powerful but adds engineering cost; start with commit–reveal + audits and add ZK later.

## Performance, cost & scalability optimizations

* **Batch transactions**: aggregate many commit publishes into a single bundle or use a rollup aggregator to reduce gas.
* **Use L2**: Polygon zkEVM / Optimism / Arbitrum to keep micro-payments feasible.
* **Use off-chain matching + on-chain settlement** to reduce smart contract complexity & gas and enable complex optimizers.
* **Gas abstraction & meta-tx:** use relayers so Pi nodes can transact without holding native chain tokens; relayer pays gas and is reimbursed in stablecoin via signed voucher.
* **Timeout windows & grace windows**: allow off-chain match signing and chaining to prevent stalled trades.

## Security hardening (must-do checklist)

* Private keys: secure element / TPM / encrypted keystore + OS hardening.
* Signatures: EIP-712 typed data signing for clarity & replay protection.
* Rate limiting & anti-sybil: identity registry + collateral deposit requirements.
* Smart contract security: OpenZeppelin libs, formal verification for critical functions, unit tests, fuzzing, and third-party audit prior to production.
* On-chain upgrades: use proxy pattern (Transparent or UUPS) with governance timelock for safe upgrades.
* Penetration testing & red-team runs on relayers and Pi firmware.

## Example minimal on-chain flow (sequence)

1. Pi A: `commit = sha256(offer)` → `TSC.postCommit(commit)` (tx)
2. Pi A: broadcast off-chain P2P offer to neighbors
3. Optimizer: reads commits → computes matching → `MatchRegistry.submitMatch(signedMatchBlob)`
4. TSC: `finalizeMatching(matchBlob)`, locks funds, emits `TradeExecuted` events
5. Pi A: hears `TradeExecuted`, runs safety shield, performs actuation → posts `DeliveryReceipt(tx)`
6. TSC: `executeSettlement(trade_id)` → funds move to seller.

---

## Final notes & tradeoffs

* **Hybrid matching + L2 settlement** offers the best practical balance of latency, cost, and transparency.
* **Privacy vs transparency:** commit–reveal gives privacy for quantities; ZK proofs can be added later for strong privacy guarantees.
* **Security first:** protect node keys and salt files; never transmit salts.
* **Start small, iterate:** begin with commit–reveal + off-chain matching + testnet stablecoin; add ZK once the loop is stable.

# The whole blockchain workflow — **Best 8 steps (production-grade, concise)**

Each step describes actor(s), what’s on-chain vs off-chain, and required security checks.

### 1) Node Onboarding & Identity (one-time)

* Pi generates keypair (secp256k1) and stores private key in secure element / TPM; derive `node_id = keccak256(pubkey|salt)` and submit `registerNode(pubkey_hash, metaURI)` to **IdentitySC**.
* Off-chain KYC or PII stored in permissioned registry; on-chain store only URI or hash. IdentitySC assigns stake address and reputation init.
* Security: verify device attestation (secure element certificate) before registration.

### 2) Collateral & Gas Setup

* Node deposits stablecoin (USDC) or posts bonded stake to **CollateralSC** / Escrow. For gas abstraction, node registers a relayer allowance: `approveRelayer(relayer_address, daily_limit)` and issues signed gas-voucher templates (EIP-712).
* Security: deposit enforces minimal solvency; relayer uses voucher nonces + signature checks to prevent replay.

### 3) Local Sensing → Offer Creation (off-chain commit & signature)

* Pi's TC composes structured offer: `{round_id, node_id_hash, offered_kwh_bucket, price_bucket, nonce, expiry_block}`. Use **buckets** or quantized values to limit info leakage and gas.
* Pi computes `offer_hash = keccak256(abi.encodePacked(struct))`, stores `salt` locally (POSIX 600 on device or secure element), and signs `offer_hash` using EIP-712 typed data.
* Pi publishes only the **commit** on chain: `TradingSC.postCommit(node_id_hash, offer_commit_hash, round_id, expiry_block)` or to an off-chain bulletin (P2P) — commit includes a small gas-efficient index. (No raw meter or salt on chain.)

### 4) P2P & Off-chain Matching (deterministic, auditable)

* Off-chain optimizer(s) (could be a federated set) read commits + available off-chain offers (P2P gossip), then run deterministic matching (LP or auction) for `round_id`.
* Optimizer outputs `MatchResult {round_id, matches[], optimizer_pubkey, signature, inputs_hash}`. Publish `inputs_hash` (commit hashes sorted) so the computation is reproducible.
* Verification model: require `k-of-n` optimizer signatures or allow challengers to post an alternative match within a short `challenge_window` (on-chain). If unchallenged, match is accepted.

### 5) On-chain Match Registration & Pre-Settlement Checks

* Submit `MatchResult` to **MatchRegistry.submitMatch(match_blob, signature)`**. Contract verifies optimizer signature(s), `round_id` monotonicity, and that all involved commits exist and are unspent.
* Contract locks buyer collateral via `CollateralSC.lockFunds(trade_id)` (atomic call) and emits `TradeProposed(trade_id, buyer, seller, qty_bucket, price_bucket)`.
* Security: verify nonces, expiry_block, and enforce stake slashing rules for invalid matches.

### 6) Reveal (if used) and Finalization / Settlement

* During reveal window, involved nodes reveal `offer_data + salt` via `TradingSC.revealOffer(node_id_hash, offer_data, salt)`. Contract verifies `keccak256(offer_hash|salt) == commit_hash`. If using quantized buckets, verify consistency.
* If reveal not required (you use buckets + signed commitments + match verification), the contract can skip reveal and proceed.
* Once checks pass, `TradingSC.executeSettlement(trade_id)` transfers stablecoin from buyer escrow to seller escrow (or marks pending until delivery confirmation). Emit `TradeExecuted(trade_id, buyer, seller, qty, price)`.

### 7) Edge Actuation & Delivery Reporting

* Pi listens to `TradeExecuted` events (filter by `node_id_hash`) via gateway/relayer or light client; runs safety shield checks (SoC, inverter limits). If safe → actuate (discharge/charge). If unsafe → emit `ActionOverride(trade_id, reason)` and optionally trigger fallback settlement.
* After delivery window, Pi posts signed `DeliveryReceipt(trade_id, meter_snapshot_hash, period, signer)` to **DeliveryRegistry** (prefer hashed/anchored receipts). Optionally anchor aggregated receipts on IPFS and store hash on chain.

### 8) Settlement Finalization, Audit & Dispute Resolution

* On proof of delivery (validated receipts + optional oracle attestations), `TradingSC` releases escrow to seller. Emit `SettlementCompleted(trade_id)`.
* If dispute raised within `dispute_window`, lock settlement and trigger dispute process: both parties submit evidence (signed meter snapshots, peer attestations, verifier attestations). A judge (on-chain DAO, appointed arbitrators, or automated verifier) resolves and slashes or reallocates collateral. Provide appeal mechanism and timeouts.
* Keep audit trail: store commits, matches, settlement events, and proof hashes on chain; raw telemetry remains off-chain.

# Minimal contract API checklist (compact)

* `IdentitySC.registerNode(pubkey_hash, metaURI)`
* `CollateralSC.deposit(node, amount) / lockFunds(trade_id, amount)`
* `TradingSC.postCommit(node_hash, commit_hash, round_id)`
* `MatchRegistry.submitMatch(match_blob, signatures)`
* `TradingSC.revealOffer(node_hash, offer_data, salt)` (optional)
* `TradingSC.executeSettlement(trade_id)`
* `DeliveryRegistry.postReceipt(trade_id, receipt_hash, signature)`
* `DisputeSC.raiseDispute(trade_id, evidence_hash)` + governance resolve

# Implementation & operational best practices (short list)

* Use quantized buckets for quantity & price to reduce privacy leakage and gas.
* Use EIP-712 typed data signing everywhere for clarity and replay protection.
* Use meta-tx / relayer pattern (EIP-2771 style) so Pis don’t need native gas tokens. Keep voucher nonces and expiry.
* Use timelocks & governance for upgrades (Transparent/UUPS + timelock).
* Build indexer + monitoring (TheGraph or custom) for observability and dispute evidence.
* Start with testnet L2 (Optimism/Polygon zkEVM/Arbitrum) or a permissioned consortium for pilots. Later move to public L2s.
* Begin with commit–reveal + stake, add ZK proofs only once workflow and cost model are validated.
